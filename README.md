# Physics-C-Final-Project
Dear Ms. Sharaf,

This is Esther Lee and Skylar Huang from Pd. 8/9. We plan on simulating the rotation of ionized plasma particles around magnetic field lines in a fusion reactor. From the Lorentz force equation, E=mc^2, and miscellaneous rate of reaction equations, we will calculate the frequency of hydrogen nuclei collisions using the acceleration of the particles due to the electromagnetic field. In addition, since the particles in the fusion reactor are moving rapidly, we will use the Runge-Kutta methods instead of Euler’s method as a form of linearization for our model. Our simulation will also have interactive features such as the number of magnets surrounding the reactor (to increase/decrease the magnetic field strength), adjusting the diameter of the cross-sectional area of the reactor to adjust the collision rate, and changing the amount of particles injected into the reactor. If we have time, we can add other parameters like the mass of the particles (which depends on the elements used in the reaction), the effect of sputtering from the reaction which causes the reactor to shut down, the velocity of the electrons (in opposite direction of the nuclei), or giving the particles an initial momentum.

The end goal of the simulation is to reach the energy threshold to power a city. By adjusting the parameters specified above, we can calculate the total energy generated from the reactor and see if it meets the threshold. We will also have graphs that depict dependent variables such as the average velocity and acceleration of the particles with respect to time (as the user changes independent variables on the interface).
